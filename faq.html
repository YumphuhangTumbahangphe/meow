Frequently Asked Questions about Cats

1. What do cats eat?
Cats are obligate carnivores, meaning they need a diet primarily of meat. Commercial cat food is formulated to provide the essential nutrients they need.
2. How can I train my cat?
Cats can be trained using positive reinforcement techniques. Start with basic commands and reward good behavior with treats or praise.
3. Do cats need to be groomed?
Yes, cats benefit from regular grooming to prevent matting, reduce shedding, and promote good hygiene. This includes brushing, nail trimming, and occasional baths.
4. How much exercise do cats need?
Cats need regular play and exercise to stay healthy and mentally stimulated. Providing toys and opportunities for interactive play can help meet their exercise needs.
5. Are certain plants toxic to cats?
Yes, some plants are toxic to cats if ingested. Examples include lilies, azaleas, and certain types of ferns. It's important to research and avoid plants that are harmful to cats.
